LeBron shows out in return to Cleveland

They are only 16 games into the season and the Los Angeles Lakers have had spectacular performances. These performances have been led by none other than 3 time NBA champion LeBron James. on Wednesday 21st October, the King made his return visit to his hometown Cleveland Cavaliers, where he won a championship in 2016. The Cavaliers made a tribute video in which they showed his highlights and thanked the Akron native for delivering on his promise to bring Cleveland a championship. The crowd then gave him a standing ovation to LeBron for putting an end to the 52 years without a championship.

The game would start with ten lead changes in the first quarter. LeBron scored the opening basket of the game, putting the Lakers up by 4. The game proceeded to go back and forth from there.

The Lakers managed to go on a 16-3 run in the last few minutes of the game in which they were able to cut the Cavaliers 8 point lead. Cavs player Jordan Clarkson recorded 20 points to give the team a run in which they were able to score 13 more points to get an 8 point lead. However the Cavs lead was cut to 5 points when Lakers guard Kyle Kuzma scored a three pointer to bring his team closer. Then Lebron James nailed two free throws, a three pointer and three more free throws. This gave the Lakers a 102-99 lead that they would not give up. Although LeBron missed four of twelve free throws he managed to get help form Kentavious Caldwell-Pope, who made four straight free throws to put the icing on the cake for the Lakers. Tristan Thompson, former teammate and close friend of LeBron, recorded 14 points and 15 rebounds. The Cavaliers are off to a slow start to the season making this their 13th loss out of fifteen games.

Quicken Loans arena was sold out for this game, and the crowd gave LeBron James a deserved standing ovation for his spectacular performance. He recieved a handfull of hugs and handshakes from his former teammates.
